The RB26 Swapped Munstang (Tokyo Drift 2006)

© 1967 Ford Mustang (NISMO ENGINE)

For the Fast Furious Tokyo Drift movie (The best FF movie in my opinion) in 2006 Universal studios, the ones who produced the movie, needed specially modified cars, to fit their needs for the upcoming movie. Particularly they had their eyes on the 1967 Ford Mustang. But its factory-tuned V8 didn’t have what it takes for the movie, It's not that a V8 cant handles the extra power.

Actually, all the stunt cars were powered by 403 cubic inch V8 crate engines. But in this particular instance, a proper V8 would leave no room in the engine compartment and an inline-six can provide plenty of power.

It was smart replacing the engine with an RB to allow for tuning. The RB engines are fine-tuned performance engines designed to be pushed to the extreme. So the RB26DETT engine (RB is the engine code used by Nissan to denote the engine type.

26 means 2.6 liters, D refers to the dual overhead cam setup, E refers to the electronic (multi-point) fuel injection, and TT means twin turbo.), taken from an R32 GT-R Skyline, This engine is used in all high-performance Japanese car’s. The standard engine puts out around 280 horsepower, But it’s possible, with custom modifications, to get as much as 850–1000hp. The ones that were used in the movie, were tuned to stay around 320–375 horsepower range.

But they had one big issue they needed to address if they wanted to use this particular engine, was that the twin-turbo system wouldn’t fit properly between the shock towers. Once Morris (The lead mechanic) got involved, he decided to put the original RB26 back in the R34 and swap in a different block with a custom fabricated single-turbo setup with a Sparco intercooler under the front bumper

The Skyline GT-R is a legendary 4-wheel drive car, so in order to make things work for the Mustang who has rear-wheel drive, they took a 5-speed manual transmission (FS5R30A) from a 1998 Skyline GT-S and bolted it to a Currie Enterprises 9-inch Ford rear end. This of course keeps the Mustang’s drive wheels at the rear, which is very important to a drift car.

Officially, this custom-built beauty laid down 340 horsepower at 7300 rpm and 264 lb-ft of torque at 5950 rpm to the wheels on the dyno. That’s a fair bit more than a stock Skyline GT-R, and a big step up over any original six-cylinder Mustang.


  • Standing Quarter: 13.5 seconds at 109.8 mph. (Which is fairly fast)

  • Manufacturer: Ford Motor Corporation

  • Make: Ford Mustang

  • Model: “Fastback”

  • Production Year: 1967

  • Model Status: In Production

  • Body Style: 2-door Fastback/Sportsroof

  • Engine: 2.6 L RB26DETT twin-turbo I6 Twin Cam 24 Value Skyline GT-R (transplanted from a Nissan Silvia S15 in the movie, not in real life) with Earl’s Performance steel braided line and anodized fittings; BeCool aluminum radiator

  • Body Mods: Shelby-style B-pillar scoops

  • Wheels: JDM Volk GT-7 245/35R19 front and 275/35R19 rear

  • Tires: Toyo Proxes T1R 245/35R19 front and 275/35R19 rear

  • Suspension Mods: Global West control arms and front coil overs; KYB rear shocks; Wilwood disc brakes

  • Paint Color: Highland Green with Wimbledon White Lemans Stripes
