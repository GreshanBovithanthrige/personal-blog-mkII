Story of the two 1968 Bullitt Mustangs


©️ Highland Green 1968 Mustang GT 390 Fastback


Warner Bros. sourced two Highland Green 1968 Mustang GT 390 Fastbacks from Ford Motor, with back to back serial numbers. Both Mustangs had identical engines and transmission which were 390/325HP big-block V8s and 4-speed manual transmissions. These two had beefed up engines, transmissions, suspensions, and camera mounts that were specially done for the famous 1968 Steve Mcqueen thriller "Bullitt". This particular Mustang (VIN 8R02S125559) in the picture is the one Steve Mcqueen drove in the movie. This was repaired and sold to a Warner Bros. employee after the movie which he later sold to a New Jersey detective in 1970, who sold it to Robert Kiernan.

Steve Mcqueen himself tried to buy this car from him, he felt like the car should be in the family. Actually he offered him a similar car in exchange but Kiernan declined all the offers. Mcqueen failed to find the other car before his untimely death in 1980. In 2014 this car was revealed to the general public by the original owner's son. Then in early 2018, the legendary car had its debut alongside Ford's third Bullitt Edition Mustang. In January 2020, the Mona Lisa of Mustangs was auctioned off to a record-setting 3.74 million, making it the most valuable Ford Mustang in the world.

On the other hand, after being lost for almost 40 years, the second Mustang (VIN 8R02S125558) has been found in a junkyard in Mexico. The car is now restored and in its original condition. Now it's estimated value is more than 1 million dollars. (The stunt car used for jumps).


  • Car - 1968 Ford Mustang Fastback

  • Engine - 390/325HP big-block V8 with a four-barrel carburetor (With custom machined heads and hotter ignition)

  • Transmission - four-speed manual transmission (With custom mods) Suspension - Koni adjustable shocks (Custom)

  • Reinforced Shock Towers

  • Heavy-duty front springs

  • Thicker anti-roll bar Wheels - American Racing Torq Thrust wheels

  • Color - Highland Green
